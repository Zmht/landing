----IN PROGRESS----Mortal Design

An Abstract Case for Beauty

by Zachary Hansen Terry

September 28th, 2024

My mortality and I have a mutual understanding.

I may ignore him without consequence and therefore I am immortal.

However, this is merely an emulation. Nothing is immortal and no emulation is perfectly accurate. It's more accurate to describe myself as halfway between immortal and mortal. I consider my situation similar to how light can be described as a wave or a particle. As a particle, light's position is ambiguous but its energy is discrete. As a wave, light diffracts and interferes but has momentum like a particle. Abstractly, I believe that the most accurate way to describe myself is through a duality. It is deeper than my emulation, which allows me to justify decisions which a mortal man wouldn't be able to justify. Not that these decisions are unique. I make the same decisions as everyone else but I think that the way I arrive at my decisions could be unique.

It is only as an immortal being that I personally can consider the future and understand long-term commitments. If I was perfectly mortal I would have no justification for the delay of satisfaction. If I thought mortality was the only force to apply to me, I would act on any and every impulse. I would desperately claw my way through life, taking what I desired, maximizing every experience, milking the world of all it has to offer me and leaving nothing. What does the world have to offer a human?

  • Love
  • Beauty
  • Humour
  • Wonder

Could a 100% immortal man appreciate any of this? I would argue that only a mortal could understand these concepts. Immortals don't need our world's offerings. They have their immortality and that knowledge is a satisfaction none of unknown to a mortal (mere mortal).

Eight billion people live here and my location: somewhere between mortal and immortal is still unique. I'm not sure where that position is. I only know that it does exist and it has to exist on that line.

It's fascinating to me that my entire state can be described by a singular point on a one dimensional line. A 6th grader could perfectly mathematically model me.

Where can I move from this line? This line of reasoning: should it fill me with despair? Should I try to modify my position on this line further, perhaps I can leave the line altogether? What if my position is also the intersection between my mortality-immortality line and another line? I'm not going to leave my position and I'm not going to try to change. If I wasn't comfortable with my mortality-immortality position, the severity of its impact on my life would be crushing, immobilizing. I do still recognize it's impact on my life, on many experiences.

Design is something I care deeply about and I think it has a unique access port into my brain. Beautiful design can reach me at any time—I'm more mentally available for a given beautiful design than for any person in my life. This is a true relationship which I have with design. Unfortunately, it is a one-sided relationship. I've never been able to design beauty. In my attempts at UI/UX, art, and decorating, I always fall short of beauty (landing squarely at hideous).

Design's intersections with mortality and immortality contribute to it's magnetic draw on my attention. My mortality is fascinated by design however so is my immortality. I think that this suggests something magical about design, a way that some designs defy boundaries between physical and spiritual.

An excellent design is immortal? An excellent design may genuinely live forever. I love to imagine this: a beautiful design which is truly (geometrically, physically, artistically) beautiful will be beautiful for the rest of humanity. Will anything else last this long?
